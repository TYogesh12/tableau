Medical Definition

Heart disease is a group of cardiovascular conditions involving functional violations of the heart. It may be caused by damage to the epicardium, myocardium, endocardium or heart vessels.

Risk Factor Analysis

According to the National Heart, Lung and Blood Institute, the most important development factors are obesity, sedentary lifestyle and smoking.
